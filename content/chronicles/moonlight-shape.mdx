Moonlight is not a color. It is a shape — the negative space around the trees,
cut out of the sky. You can trace its edges with a finger if you stand still
enough.

I tried to photograph it last winter and learned that cameras refuse the
assignment. The sensor flattens the light into something polite and grey. You
need eyes, and a patient body, and the discipline to not blink for a while.

## What it does to the river

Where the moonlight lands on the river there is a brief second-glow — a
silver layered on top of the cyan, the way two glazes stack on a ceramic. It
only happens at the widest section, where the surface flattens out and the
water forgets to keep moving.

I have started calling that section the **bridge**, even though there is no
bridge there.
