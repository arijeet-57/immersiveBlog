The river does not raise its voice. It runs cyan under the moss and waits for
you to lean in. If you put your hand near the surface you can feel the cold
before you feel the water.

I have been told that what glows in the silt is plankton, that there is a
chemical explanation, that the river is "doing exactly what rivers do." I am
not interested in being correct about it.

## Notes from the bank

- The brightest patches are on the inside of the bends, where the current slows.
- A single firefly will pace you upstream for half a mile if you walk slowly enough.
- The far bank disappears into fog before the trees do — like a curtain dropping
  in front of a curtain.
