A small blue light opened in the dark and I forgot what I had come here to look
for. The petals were thinner than I expected — almost translucent — and the
white starburst at the center pulsed once, twice, like something deciding
whether to trust me.

I had walked this meadow a hundred times in daylight and never seen one. They
only bloom when nobody is keeping count.

## What I learned that night

It is possible to live in a place for years and never meet it. The world is
generous, but it does not announce itself. You have to put the camera down,
sit on the wet ground, and wait until the air forgets you are there.
