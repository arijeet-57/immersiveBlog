I lost the path for almost an hour. The trees were tall enough that even the
moon was a rumor — a pale color happening above the canopy, never inside it.

I have never been afraid in this forest, which is its own kind of strange. The
fear belongs somewhere else. Here there is only the slow inventory of which
direction is uphill and which way the wind is going.

## A list of small noises

1. A branch settling, far away, into a different branch.
2. Something small turning over a leaf, deciding against it, going back to sleep.
3. The trunk I was leaning on shifting under its own weight in a way I felt
   more than heard.

When I found the path again it was three feet to my left and had been there
the whole time.
