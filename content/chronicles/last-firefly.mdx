I waited at the edge of the meadow until the air had nothing left to say. The
last firefly was small and stubborn — a single warm point against the cold
blue field, refusing to leave until I did.

I have an old habit of staying out past my own bedtime to keep some small
creature company. It is, almost certainly, only company in one direction. The
firefly does not need me. The firefly is, in fact, looking for a different
firefly entirely, and would be relieved if I left.

## On staying anyway

There is a version of attention that is closer to being present than to
watching. You are not the audience. You are simply another body in the
field — slower, larger, warmer in a way the firefly cannot see.

When it finally lifted off I did not follow.
