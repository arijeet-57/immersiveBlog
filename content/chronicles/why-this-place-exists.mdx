Hi. I'm Arro.

If you've found this place, you've probably already wandered through the
meadow, drifted over the river, and lost yourself somewhere in the dark forest
on the way to the moonlit valley. I'm glad you're here.

## Who I am

I'm not a poet, and I'm not really a writer. I'm someone who thinks too much
and talks too little, and a lot of what I want to say doesn't fit anywhere
ordinary — not in a tweet, not in a status update, not in a conversation that's
already moving too fast. The thoughts I care about are quiet ones. They need
some space around them to be heard.

## Why I built this

For a long time I kept those thoughts in a notebook and never let anyone see
them. Then I started wondering whether keeping them entirely to myself was a
kind of cowardice — whether the small honest things I noticed might mean
something to someone else, if I gave them a place to land.

So I made a valley.

I wanted somewhere that didn't feel like a blog. Blogs are bright and tidy
and they want you to read fast and move on. The things I write here aren't
like that. They're slow, and a little strange, and they're better at twilight.
I wanted the *place* to feel that way too — like you wandered into it by
accident, like the light is wrong in the way good things sometimes are.

## What you'll find here

The **Chronicles** are field notes. Small moments I noticed. A flower that
opened when I wasn't paying attention. A thought I had standing in a river.
The kinds of things that are usually too small to say out loud, and so they
disappear.

The **Sanctuary** is a quieter room. It exists for the few people who want to
sit with me a little longer than a passing visit. There isn't much in there
yet — there will be, in time.

The **Whispers** are for you. If something here moves you, or if you have your
own small honest thing to leave behind, you can. I'll read it. That's a
promise.

## A small request

If you stay a while, let the page sit. Let the butterfly come back around.
Don't try to absorb it all in one pass — there isn't a finish line. This
place is meant to be returned to, not finished.

Thanks for being here.

— Arro
